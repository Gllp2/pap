Components

Before assembling a PC, we need all the necessary parts for it.

On this page, I will show you what these components are.

Tip: Hover your cursor over each image to see more details.

Motherboard

The motherboard is the main circuit board of the PC. Here, we install the CPU, memory, cables for the hard drive, and other components like video and audio cards. It's also where ports like USB and HDMI are housed.

The PCB (printed circuit board) of larger motherboards can include six to 14 layers of fiberglass, copper connection traces, and copper planes for power delivery and signal isolation. Other components are added to the motherboard via expansion slots. This includes processor sockets; dual in-line memory modules; Peripheral Component Interconnect (PCI), PCI Express (PCIe), and M.2 slots for solid-state drives; as well as power supply connections.

* It's an insulating board that has a thin layer of copper on the surface with conductive traces that connect various components.
PCIe Ports

CPU/Processor

The Central Processing Unit (CPU), also known as the processor, is inserted into a socket on the motherboard. We can call the CPU the brain of the PC, as it processes all our commands. Whenever you press a key, click a mouse, or open an application, you send instructions to the CPU.

The processor speed is measured in megahertz (MHz) and gigahertz (GHz). A faster processor can execute instructions more quickly. However, the actual speed of the computer depends on the speed of many different components – not just the processor.

GPU

A GPU is an electronic circuit used to process the images and videos you see. Not only that, but also any games you play that require GPU precision. If a game is set to maximum graphics settings, your graphics card might not be strong enough to handle it.

Graphics Card

RAM

RAM is your system's short-term memory. Whenever your computer calculates something, it temporarily stores the data in RAM until it's needed. This short-term memory disappears when the computer is turned off. If you're working on a document or file, you'll need to save it to avoid losing it. When you save a file, the data is written to the hard drive, which functions as long-term storage.

RAM is measured in megabytes (MB) or gigabytes (GB). The more RAM you have, the more your computer can do at once. If you don't have enough RAM, you may notice your computer slowing down when multiple programs are open. If that's the case, consider adding more RAM to your PC to improve performance.

SSD

Solid-state drives (SSDs) are the most common storage drives today. SSDs are smaller and faster than hard disk drives (HDDs). SSDs are noiseless and allow PCs to be thinner and more lightweight. Hard disk drives (HDDs) are more common in older devices.

SSD

Power Supply

A computer's power supply unit converts electricity for the PC. It sends power through cables to the motherboard and other components.

Power Supply Unit

Extras

Sound Card (Expansion)

The sound card – also called an audio card – is responsible for what you hear through speakers or headphones. Most motherboards have integrated sound card, but if you dont you can upgrade to a dedicated sound card for higher quality audio.

Sound Card Expansion

Network Card (Expansion)

A network card enables your computer to access the internet. It can be connected via Ethernet cable or Wi-Fi. Most motherboards have integrated network connections, and a network card can also be added to an expansion slot.

Network Card Expansion

Bluetooth

Bluetooth is a technology for wireless communication over short distances. It's often used in computers for communication with wireless keyboards, mice, and printers. It's usually integrated into the motherboard or included in a wireless network card. For computers without Bluetooth, you can purchase a USB adapter, often called a dongle.

Bluetooth Adapter

Termalpaste

The main role of thermal paste is to eliminate air gaps or spaces (which act as thermal insulation) from the interface area in order to maximize heat transfer and dissipation. Thermal paste is an example of a thermal interface material.
